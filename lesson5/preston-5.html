Cool Weather Vegetable Gardening

By Ellen Bell

If you're like most gardeners, you probably can't wait for the weather to warm up so you can get outside and start planting. Particularly in the case of vegetable gardening, most plants can't go in the ground until the weather warms up significantly and the danger of frost has passed.

But have you considered planting some cool weather vegetable crops that can be sown as early as several weeks before the last expected frost date? Cool weather vegetables are a great way to get a head start on the season's plantings. Not only do cool weather veggies keep your planting areas productive for more months of the year, they also allow you to get out in the garden earlier in the spring.

In this article, we'll explore a few varieties of cool weather vegetables that you might want to try growing in your garden this spring.
